NBA Injuries EDA¶

The following document analyzes player injuries in the NBA from the years 2010 to 2020. A point of contention in the NBA is how often players are rested when they may or may not even be injuried. This analysis looks at both injuries and rest in the context of missed games. The data was pulled from Kaggle.

Data Source:

  • injuries_2010-2020.csv

Below is a list of variables that were of importance:

  • date
  • team
  • acquired
  • notes

Visualizing the average number of players who rested per year¶

In [65]:
group_year = nba_injuries.iloc[index_of_rest].groupby(nba_injuries.Date.dt.year)['Notes']
group_year.count()
plt.plot(group_year.count(), linewidth=3, marker='o')
plt.title('Number of players who rested per year')
plt.xlabel('Year')
plt.ylabel('Number of players who rested')
Out[65]:
Text(0, 0.5, 'Number of players who rested')

The graph above shows us how many players rested per year from 2011-2018. While there is an upward trend, from this we can see that the number of players who rested peaked in 2016 and has fallen since. Player rest or 'Load Management' has been a hot debate in NBA circles as fans have become increasingly upset that players have been steadily taking more games off. From the graph above we can see that their sentiments are true that players have been 'resting' more and more. However, it seems like this pushback from fans is what may have caused this decline in players resting since 2016.

From this graph we can get a better sense as to when a majority of players are taking their rest. We can see an upward trend from the start of the season (October) to right before the playoffs (April) in the number of players who rest per month. This makes sense as more star players begin to rest before the Playoffs in order to stay healthy for them. Early April/Late March makes sense as the peak of time in which players would rest as this is right before the playoffs start. October also makes sense for being the month in which players rest the least as it is start/first month of the season.
